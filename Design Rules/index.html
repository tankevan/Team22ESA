Design Rules

Design Rules Rationale
1 Components should only interact with other components if described in the DSM To prevent unplanned effects of user actions; also allows for easier debugging when something goes wrong
2 Components that have more than 2 sub-systems should be split into separate components During development, more features could be discovered to be necessary in each component. To keep a clear separation of functions, the component should be split into two or more components.
3 Changes to component interactions due to clarifications should be updated in the DSM Teams should have the ability to update the DSM to reflect newly-found/redundant interactions in order for the DSM to accurately describe the problem
4 All teams are to be notified of any changes in the DSM Keeps everyone in the loop of architectural changes so that they can adjust their plans accordingly. It is not likely for teams to be overloaded with information due to the limited number of components. This measure is necessary to prevent any unintended effects
5 Work iteratively Implementing the functionality of a component at a low-resolution will help to validate the interaction between components quickly and help to determine if the team has made any architectural oversights early in the process
6 All graphical assets shall be a 20x20px iamge drawn in the style of pixel art
7 All trash should be at most 30% of the user's avatar
